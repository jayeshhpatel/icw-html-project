GIA Certifications Diamonds

The International Gemmological Institute (IGI) tests and grades gemstones, assessing diamonds based on cut, clarity, color, and carat weight according to strict international standards. At Ecolight, our diamonds are IGI certified, undergoing thorough examination across the 4C’s to ensure they meet these high standards.

Each certified diamond comes with a detailed report outlining its unique characteristics, including a clarity diagram for transparency. We ensure that our lab-grown diamonds meet IGI standards, providing our clients with confidence in their purchase. Fancy Diamonds

Fancy-shaped diamonds are those with shapes other than round brilliant, such as oval, princess, cushion, emerald, pear, marquise, Asscher, and heart. The shape of a diamond is determined by its physical form. In contrast, fancy-colored diamonds go beyond the normal color range (D-Z) and include hues like yellow, brown, blue, red, green, and pink.

While fancy-colored diamonds may cost less than pure white ones, their value increases with the strength and purity of the color. Larger diamonds with intense fancy colors are rare and often more expensive due to their unique appeal.

Stars and Melee Diamonds

Star and melee diamonds are tiny crystals used to accentuate larger brilliants. Melee diamonds, as defined by the GIA, weigh less than 0.2 carats, with some as small as 0.001 carats. Star diamonds, also called diamond dust, range from 0.18 to 0.99 carats, with the largest measuring around 1.8mm.

Melee diamonds can be cut in two ways: Single Cut with 17 facets or Full Cut with 57-58 facets, similar to larger brilliants. While these small diamonds are not sold individually, they are valued for their craftsmanship and are typically sold in parcels, with pricing based on size, cut, and weight.
